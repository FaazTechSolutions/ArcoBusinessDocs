Labour Contract Management Business Guide

1. Introduction

This system is used to manage a labour supply business.

The company provides house workers or labour workers to customers for a fixed number of days or months through contracts.

The complete business includes:

  • Customer registration
  • Labour package setup
  • Labour booking
  • Employee assignment
  • Delivery to customer
  • Employee return process
  • Contract extension
  • Cancellation and refund
  • Finance and payment tracking

2. Simple Business Flow Overview

The overall business works like this:

  1. Company creates labour packages
  2. Customer registers in the system
  3. Customer selects package and contract period
  4. Customer makes payment
  5. Contract is created
  6. Employee is assigned
  7. Employee is delivered to customer
  8. Contract days are consumed
  9. Employee may return / exchange / terminate
  10. Finance settlement is completed

4. Understanding the Core Business

This company supplies house workers to customers.

Example:

  • Customer wants a housemaid for 90 days
  • Customer selects a package
  • Customer pays advance amount
  • Company assigns an employee
  • Employee works at customer location
  • Contract days are reduced daily

If employee returns:

  • Customer can request replacement
  • Or terminate the contract
  • Or extend the contract

This is the complete business cycle.

5. Package Module

What is a Package?​

Package means:

  • Labour type
  • Pricing
  • Contract duration
  • Payment structure

Example:

Package Types​

1. Monthly Low-Cost Package​

Customer pays:

  • Advance amount
  • Monthly installment

Used for:

  • Long-term labour contracts

2. Per-Day Package​

Pricing depends on:

  • Number of days
  • Worker category
  • Nationality

Example:

6. Customer Module

Customer Registration​

Customer can register from:

  • Mobile App
  • Branch Office

Customer details include:

  • Name
  • Mobile Number
  • ID Number
  • Nationality
  • Address
  • Family Information
  • Bank Details

Customer Delegation​

Sometimes another person manages the contract.

Example:

  • Husband creates contract
  • Wife handles worker communication

That person is stored as:

  • Delegation Contact

7. Opportunity Module

Opportunity means:

  • Customer interest before actual contract creation

Simple understanding:

  • Temporary booking request

Opportunity Flow​

  1. Customer selects package
  2. Customer selects days
  3. System calculates amount
  4. Customer reserves employee (optional)
  5. Customer pays amount
  6. Contract gets created automatically

Important Rule​

If payment is not completed within 20 minutes:

  • Opportunity automatically cancels

8. Contract Module

Contract is the official agreement between:

  • Company
  • Customer

The contract controls:

  • Package
  • Duration
  • Employee
  • Payments
  • Delivery
  • Extensions
  • Refunds

9. Contract Status Understanding

This is the most important business logic.

1. New​

Contract created. Payment pending.

2. Document Verification​

Customer completed payment. Documents/signature verification pending.

3. Waiting for Selection​

Customer must select employee.

4. Waiting for Delivery​

Employee selected. Delivery process pending.

5. Valid​

Employee is working with customer. Contract is active.

6. Waiting for Exchange​

Employee returned. Customer can choose replacement employee.

7. Near to Expire​

Remaining contract days less than 15.

8. Expired​

Contract days completed.

9. Ended​

Contract closed normally.

10. Terminated​

Customer closed contract before completion.

11. Cancelled​

Contract cancelled before employee delivery.

10. Employee Assignment Process

Simple Workflow​

  1. Contract enters Waiting for Selection
  2. User selects employee from lodging
  3. Employee status changes to Booked
  4. Contract moves to Waiting for Delivery
  5. Delivery process starts

Important Rule​

Employee can be unassigned before delivery.

If unassigned:

  • Before 24 hours → no charge
  • After 24 hours → charge applicable

11. Labour Delivery Process

Delivery Methods​

MethodMeaning
Pick from LodgeCustomer collects employee
Pick from BranchCustomer collects from branch
Delivery to HomeCompany sends employee home

Delivery Flow​

  1. Payment collection
  2. Employee checkout from lodging
  3. Travel arrangement
  4. Customer confirmation
  5. Employee delivered
  6. Contract becomes Valid

12. Employee Return Process

Why Employee Returns?​

Possible reasons:

  • Replacement request
  • Vacation
  • Escape
  • Termination
  • End of contract

Return Methods​

MethodDescription
Return to LodgeEmployee comes directly
Pickup from HomeCompany arranges pickup

13. Replacement Process

When it happens?​

Customer is unhappy with employee.

Company provides another employee.

Important Rule​

System allows limited free replacements.

After limit exceeds:

  • Replacement fee applies

14. Extend Process

Customer wants more contract days.

Example:

  • Existing contract = 90 days
  • Customer extends 30 more days

System:

  • Creates payment request
  • Adds new contract days

15. Cancel vs Terminate

Cancel​

Meaning:

  • Employee not yet delivered
  • Customer cancels contract

Penalty:

  • Cancellation fee applies

Terminate​

Meaning:

  • Employee already worked
  • Customer stops contract early

Penalty:

  • Termination fee applies

16. Escape Process

Employee leaves customer location without approval.

System actions:

  • Escape request created
  • Employee removed from contract
  • Contract moves to Waiting for Exchange

17. Finance Module Simplified

Finance module manages:

  • Payments
  • Invoices
  • Refunds
  • Wallet
  • Advance amounts
  • Penalties

18. Advance Payment

Customer pays advance amount before contract activation.

Advance payment created during:

  • Contract creation
  • Contract extension

19. Invoice Types

Invoice TypePurpose

20. Wallet System

What is Wallet?​

Customer balance stored inside system.

Wallet can be used for:

  • New contracts
  • Extensions
  • Future payments

Wallet contains:

  • Refundable amount
  • Non-refundable amount

21. Refund Process

Refund happens during:

  • Cancellation
  • Termination
  • Wallet withdrawal

Approval stages:

  1. Accountant Approval
  2. Finance Manager Approval
  3. Refund Processing
  4. Completion

22. Promotion System

Discount offers for customers.

Example:

  • Ramadan offer
  • Referral code
  • Marketing campaign

Promotion Types​

TypePurpose
AffiliateReferral rewards
MarketingGeneral campaigns

23. VIP Customer System

Premium customer benefit program.

VIP customers may get:

  • Discounts
  • Skip penalties
  • Pay later facility
  • Special delivery options

24. Branch and Lodging

Branch​

Business office location.

Lodging​

Location where employees stay before assignment.

Employees move:

  • Lodge → Customer
  • Customer → Lodge

25. Credit Control

To stop customers with unpaid dues from taking new employees.

Restriction happens when:

  • Contract expired
  • Due amount exceeds limit

26. Sadad Payment Integration

External bill payment system.

System automatically:

  • Creates Sadad bill
  • Sends bill to customer
  • Receives payment confirmation
  • Creates payment entry

27. Security Module

Security controls:

  • User access
  • Roles
  • Permissions
  • Module visibility

Example:

  • Finance user cannot access HR setup
  • Branch user cannot approve refunds

29. Important Business Understanding for Freshers

Key Point 1​

Everything revolves around:

  • Contract
  • Employee
  • Customer

Key Point 2​

Employee movement is the core operational activity.

Key Point 3​

Finance settlement is tightly connected with:

  • Contract status
  • Delivery
  • Extensions
  • Refunds

Key Point 4​

  • Status
  • Approval
  • Finance impact

30. Final Summary

This business is basically:

"Supplying workers to customers through controlled contracts, tracked employee movement, and finance settlement workflows."

The system manages:

  • Customer lifecycle

  • Delivery lifecycle
  • Refund lifecycle

All modules are connected together.
